\rowcolor{ice}Nature analogy & Software meaning & Role in learning \\
Food & Unlabeled 64 x 64 retinal patches and the residual information they contain. & Novel residuals sustain adaptation; familiar input offers progressively less useful food. \\
Cell & A small learned adaptive unit or prototype. & Encodes a local visual regularity and contributes to an organism. \\
Organism & A compact, simulated local expert made from cells. & Grows and specializes where its contribution improves representation. \\
Micro-signature & An intermediate specialist for fine contours and local detail. & Helps separate globally similar forms, such as circles and squares. \\
Colony & Cooperation among organisms when shared structure is useful. & Combines complementary specialists without forcing every organism into one monolith. \\
Memory engram & Consolidated familiar structure. & Preserves learned organization and reduces the need to keep growing on known input. \\
\end{tabularx}

\sectiontitle{A demand-driven alternative to fixed capacity}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries\color{navy}}p{1.55in} X X}
\rowcolor{softamber}Question & Conventional fixed architecture & ColonyMind hypothesis \\
When is capacity chosen? & Mostly before training through layer width, depth, and parameter count. & During learning, in response to persistent information residuals. \\
Where does specialization live? & Distributed across a predetermined topology. & In inspectable cells, organism experts, micro-signatures, colonies, and memories. \\
What happens when input is familiar? & The full configured network still executes. & Useful food declines; growth should stabilize and consolidated structure can be reused. \\
How is change studied? & Retrain or replace a model configuration. & Preserve an immutable baseline and run bounded variants in fresh isolated engines. \\
\end{tabularx}

\sectiontitle{How computational efficiency is meant to emerge}
The system follows a demand-driven capacity rule: allocate structure while
unexplained information remains, reward only useful contribution, consolidate
stable structure into memory, and reduce births when the stream is already
digested.

\vspace{4pt}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{X X X}
\rowcolor{ice}\centering\bfseries CAPACITY & \centering\bfseries ADAPTATION & \centering\arraybackslash\bfseries REUSE\\
Avoid reserving the final architecture in advance. & Concentrate new structure where residuals persist. & Preserve familiar structure instead of repeatedly relearning it.\\
\end{tabularx}

\vspace{8pt}
\colorbox{softviolet}{\parbox{\dimexpr\textwidth-2\fboxsep\relax}{
\kicker{The testable hypothesis}\par
If useful residual food declines, births and structural updates should decline
with it while predictive quality remains stable. If novelty returns, growth
should resume where the new residual appears. This can be compared directly
with a matched fixed-capacity baseline.
}}

\sectiontitle{What is measured today}
The application records cell and organism populations, structural births,
prototype updates, colony formation, memory consolidation, loss behavior, and
evaluation quality. These make the growth policy inspectable and support
controlled comparisons between versioned experiments.

\vspace{5pt}
\colorbox{softamber}{\parbox{\dimexpr\textwidth-2\fboxsep\relax}{
\kicker{What is not proven yet}\par
Current resource values are engineering \textbf{proxies}. A controlled
benchmark must still measure wall-clock time, peak memory, FLOPs or accelerator
utilization, and physical energy against matched fixed-network baselines before
claiming real computational or energy savings.
}}

\sectiontitle{A focused two-minute judge path}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Observe learning.} Let the retina advance. Relate incoming
  information food to births, growth, micro-signatures, colonies, and memories.
  Watch the digestion light move as residual food changes.
  \item \textbf{Explore structure.} Rotate and zoom the 3D field. Select one
  organism and inspect its tissue, lifecycle, lineage, affinities, and colony.
  \item \textbf{Challenge recognition.} Draw a shape in Draw \& Audit. Check
  whether the learned community and external geometric auditor agree.
  \item \textbf{Reveal evaluation.} Run the hidden-label read-only evaluation.
  Labels are never used for training; they exist only in the frozen evaluator.
  \item \textbf{Ask GPT-5.6.} Click \textbf{Audit run}. Before and after state
  hashes must match. A fresh request may take 30 to 60 seconds; a repeated state
  can return from cache.
  \item \textbf{Version the next question.} Add an optional constraint and
  create an isolated protocol. Baseline v1 remains locked and no generated code
  is executed.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{4pt}

$\rightarrow$ 3D architecture $\rightarrow$ selected organism $\rightarrow$
Draw \& Audit $\rightarrow$ evaluator $\rightarrow$ GPT-5.6 audit
$\rightarrow$ versioned experiment.

\sectiontitle{How to interpret the metrics}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries\color{navy}}p{1.1in} p{1.42in} X}
\rowcolor{softviolet}Metric & Better direction & What it answers\\
Purity & Higher & Do hidden categories dominate individual learned communities? Purity alone can reward fragmentation.\\
NMI & Higher & How completely does the full community assignment align with hidden categories?\\
ARI & Higher & How well do sample pairs agree after correcting for chance?\\
Fragmentation & Near 1.0 & Does one hidden category require many communities instead of a compact representation?\\
Loss trend & Lower / stable & Is recent reconstruction improving under the system's own loss definition?\\
Resource score & Context only & Engineering proxy for structure and activity - not watts, FLOPs, or measured physical memory.\\
\end{tabularx}

\sectiontitle{Why GPT-5.6 cannot alter the result}
The Research Auditor receives aggregate metrics, structural counts, evaluation
evidence, and declared limitations. It receives no raw retina, drawings, cell
prototypes, mutable engine reference, tools, or learning endpoint. The
Experiment Designer may emit only a schema-validated allowlisted protocol; each
version runs in a fresh engine and the submitted baseline remains immutable.

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{>{\bfseries\color{navy}}p{1.18in} X >{\raggedright\arraybackslash\color{muted}}p{1.75in}}
